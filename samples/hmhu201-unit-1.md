## 1. How New Words Are Made (Word Formation)

Just like we mix ingredients to make a cake, we build new words in English using four main methods: Compounding, Backformation, Clipping, and Blending.

### A. Compounding (Sticking Words Together)
Compounding is when you take two complete words that already exist and glue them together to make a brand-new word with a new meaning.

#### Three Ways to Write Compounds:
1. **With a Space (Open):** Written as two separate words, but we read them as one idea.  
   * *Examples:* ice cream, high school, post office.
2. **Without a Space (Closed):** Smushed together into a single word.  
   * *Examples:* keyboard, notebook, butterfly, brainstorm.
3. **With a Hyphen (Hyphenated):** Connected by a small dash (-). We usually do this when they describe a noun that comes right after them.  
   * *Examples:* a well-known writer, a state-of-the-art computer, a long-term plan.

> [!TIP]
> **The Dash Rule:** If you say "a light-green jacket," use a dash because "light-green" describes the jacket. But if you say "the jacket is light green," you do not need a dash!


### B. Backformation (Working Backwards)
Usually, we make new words by adding endings (like turning the verb *write* into the noun *writer*). Backformation is the exact opposite! We take an existing noun, chop off the ending, and create a new verb.

| Original Noun | Ending We Chop Off | New Verb We Create |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Television** | -ion | **Televise** |
| **Editor** | -or | **Edit** |
| **Donation** | -ion | **Donate** |
| **Burglar** | -ar | **Burgle** |
| **Babysitter** | -er | **Babysit** |


### C. Clipping (Shortening Words)
Clipping is like giving a word a haircut. We just cut off one or more parts of the word to make it shorter, but the meaning stays exactly the same.

#### Types of Clipping:
* **Chipping off the End (Most Common):**  
  * *Examination* becomes **Exam**  
  * *Laboratory* becomes **Lab**  
  * *Advertisement* becomes **Ad**  
  * *Gymnasium* becomes **Gym**
* **Chipping off the Start:**  
  * *Telephone* becomes **Phone**  
  * *Aeroplane* becomes **Plane**
* **Chipping both Start and End:**  
  * *Influenza* becomes **Flu**  
  * *Refrigerator* becomes **Fridge**



### D. Blending (Merging Words)
Blending is when we take two words, chop off parts of both, and blend them together to make a hybrid word. 

* **Breakfast** + **Lunch** = **Brunch**
* **Smoke** + **Fog** = **Smog**
* **Motor** + **Hotel** = **Motel**
* **Information** + **Entertainment** = **Infotainment**
* **Binary** + **Digit** = **Bit**

## 2. Word Roots and Prefixes/Suffixes from Other Languages

Many English words come from ancient Latin and Greek. If you learn these building blocks, you can guess the meaning of thousands of words!

### A. Latin and Greek Roots (The Core Meaning)

| Root | Meaning | Examples & Simple Explanations |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bene / Bon** | Good, Well | **Beneficial** (doing good), **Benevolent** (kind/wishing well) |
| **Mal** | Bad, Evil | **Malevolent** (wishing bad things), **Malfunction** (working badly) |
| **Chron** | Time | **Chronological** (in time order), **Synchronize** (happen at the same time) |
| **Tele** | Far away | **Telephone** (hearing from far away), **Telescope** (looking at far away things) |
| **Spect** | Look, Watch | **Spectator** (someone watching a game), **Inspect** (look closely into something) |
| **Morph** | Shape | **Amorphous** (having no clear shape), **Metamorphosis** (changing shape) |
| **Path** | Feeling, Illness | **Sympathy** (feeling sorry for someone), **Apathy** (having no feeling/not caring) |
| **Aud** | Hear | **Audible** (loud enough to be heard), **Audience** (people who listen/watch) |
| **Cred** | Believe | **Credible** (easy to believe), **Incredulous** (unable to believe something) |
| **Loqu** | Speak | **Loquacious** (very talkative), **Eloquent** (speaking beautifully) |



### B. Prefixes (Added to the Start) and Suffixes (Added to the End)

#### Prefixes (Change the direction of a word):
* **Anti-** (against): **Antivirus** (against computer viruses), **Antisocial** (against socializing).
* **Pseudo-** (false/fake): **Pseudonym** (a fake name used by an author).
* **Inter-** (between/among): **International** (between nations).
* **Intra-** (inside/within): **Intranet** (an internal network within a single company).
* **Hyper-** (too much/excessive): **Hyperactive** (too active).
* **Hypo-** (too little/under): **Hypothermia** (having a body temperature that is too low).

#### Suffixes (Change the state or category of a word):
* **-phobia** (fear of): **Acrophobia** (fear of heights), **Claustrophobia** (fear of closed spaces).
* **-ology** (the study of): **Biology** (study of life), **Zoology** (study of animals).
* **-cide** (killing): **Pesticide** (bug killer), **Homicide** (killing another person).
* **-phile** (someone who loves): **Bibliophile** (a book lover).

## 3. Synonyms and Antonyms: Small Differences in Meaning

* **Synonyms** are words with the same or very similar meanings (like *big* and *large*).
* **Antonyms** are words with opposite meanings (like *hot* and *cold*).

> [!NOTE]
> **Word Vibes (Connotation):**  
> Even if two words are synonyms, they can give off different "vibes" or feelings:
> * **Slim / Slender:** Feels positive and healthy.
> * **Thin:** Feels neutral (just describing a fact).
> * **Skinny / Scrawny:** Can feel negative or unhealthy.
> 
> Be careful when choosing words so you don't accidentally offend someone!


## 4. Abbreviations and Acronyms

We often shorten long words or phrases to save time. But there are rules for how we do it!

* **Abbreviation:** Any short form of a word.  
  * *Examples:* Dr. (Doctor), Jan. (January).
* **Initialism:** You shorten a phrase to its first letters, and you pronounce it letter-by-letter.  
  * *Examples:* HTML (H-T-M-L), FBI (F-B-I), URL (U-R-L).
* **Acronym:** You take the first letters of a phrase, but you pronounce it like a single word.  
  * *Examples:* Laser, Scuba, NASA, Radar.

### Latin Short-hands Used in School:
You will see these abbreviations in textbooks all the time. Here is what they actually mean:

| Abbreviation | Latin Word | Meaning | How to Use It |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **e.g.** | *exempli gratia* | For example | Use it to give examples. (e.g., I like outdoor sports, e.g., soccer and tennis.) |
| **i.e.** | *id est* | In other words | Use it to clarify or explain. (i.e., We will meet on the last day of the workweek, i.e., Friday.) |
| **etc.** | *et cetera* | And other things | Put it at the end of a list to mean "and so on". (Never write "and etc.") |
| **et al.** | *et alii* | And others (people) | Used when citing a book written by a group of authors. (Smith et al. wrote the paper.) |
| **N.B.** | *nota bene* | Pay attention | Used to highlight an important note. (N.B. Bring your calculator to the exam!) |


## 5. Easy Memory Tricks

* **e.g. = "Example Given"** (Easy way to remember it means *for example*).
* **i.e. = "In Essence"** (Easy way to remember it means *in other words*).
* **Clipping is like hair clipping:** You just chop off the end to make it shorter (examination -> exam).
* **Blending is like a kitchen blender:** You throw in parts of two words to mix them together (smoke + fog -> smog).


## 6. Practice Questions

### Part A: Word Matching
Match the word on the left with the way it was made on the right:

1. **Brunch** — [ ] A. Compounding (Sticking two full words together)
2. **Phone** — [ ] B. Backformation (Subtracting an ending)
3. **Notebook** — [ ] C. Blending (Mixing parts of two words)
4. **Donate** (from *donation*) — [ ] D. Clipping (Chopping a word short)

#### Answers:
1-C, 2-D, 3-A, 4-B.


### Part B: Root Challenge
Choose the correct word to fill in the blank:

1. A device that lets you see things that are very far away is a _____________. (Telescope / Microscope)
2. A list of events arranged in order of time is in _____________ order. (Chronological / Morphological)
3. If someone is very talkative, they are _____________. (Loquacious / Benevolent)

#### Answers:
1. **Telescope** (*Tele* means far away).
2. **Chronological** (*Chron* means time).
3. **Loquacious** (*Loqu* means speak).


### Part C: e.g. vs. i.e.
Fill in the blanks with **e.g.** or **i.e.**

1. My favorite fruits are citrus fruits, _______, oranges, lemons, and limes.
2. The final exam is mandatory, _______, you must take it to pass the class.

#### Answers:
1. **e.g.** (introducing examples of citrus fruits).
2. **i.e.** (explaining what "mandatory" means in other words).
