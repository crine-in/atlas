## 1. Reading Comprehension (Understanding what you read)

Reading comprehension tests how well you understand a story or passage and how accurately you can find the answers.

### Three Quick Tips for Success:
* **Read the Questions First:** Before reading the main passage, look at the questions. This helps your brain know what details to hunt for.
* **Skim and Scan:**
   * **Skim (The Helicopter View):** Read the text super fast to get the main idea (what the story is about).
   * **Scan (The Spotlight View):** Look for specific numbers, dates, or keywords that match the questions.
* **Use Your Own Words:** When writing answers, do not just copy-paste sentences from the text. Rephrase them to show that you actually understand.

### Full Example:
Let's read this short passage and see how to answer questions correctly.

> **Passage: The Magic of Honeybees**  
> Honeybees are incredible builders. They work together in huge families called colonies to construct hives made of beeswax. To gather food, bees fly from flower to flower, collecting a sweet liquid called nectar. As they do this, they carry tiny yellow grains of pollen from plant to plant. This helpful action, called pollination, helps flowers produce seeds and grow new plants. Without the hard work of honeybees, many of the fruits and vegetables we eat today would not be able to grow.

#### Question 1: What is the sweet liquid that bees collect from flowers?
* **Bad Answer (Copy-pasting):** *"To gather food, bees fly from flower to flower, collecting a sweet liquid called nectar."* (This is lazy because it copies the book word-for-word).
* **Good Answer (Your own words):** **Bees collect a sweet juice from flowers called nectar.**

#### Question 2: Why is pollination important for plants?
* **Bad Answer (Incomplete):** *"Helps flowers produce seeds."* (Not a complete sentence).
* **Good Answer (Complete and clear):** **Pollination is important because it helps flowers make seeds so new plants can grow.**

## 2. Précis Writing (Writing a Summary)

A **précis** is a short, neat summary of a longer text. 

### The Rules of a Précis:
* **The 1/3 Rule:** It must be about **one-third (1/3)** of the size of the original text.
* **Keep it in One Paragraph:** Never use multiple paragraphs.
* **Use Third Person:** Write using *he, she, they,* or *it*. Do not write "I think" or "In my opinion."
* **Add a Title:** A précis must always have a short, catchy title at the top.
* **Leave Out the Fluff:** Delete all examples, quotes, numbers, and lists. Keep only the main point.

### Step-by-Step Précis Example:

#### The Original Passage (90 words):
> "The invention of paper by the ancient Chinese was a major turning point in human history. Before paper existed, people had to write on heavy clay tablets, expensive animal skins, or sheets of wood. These materials were very difficult to carry and make. Once paper was invented using simple tree bark and hemp, it became cheap and easy for everyone to write down stories, draw maps, and share ideas. Consequently, schools grew, books became popular, and knowledge spread quickly to people all over the world."

#### Step 1: Strike out the "Fluff" (Examples and descriptions):
* We will skip the list of old materials (*clay tablets, animal skins, wood*).
* We will skip the recipe for paper (*tree bark and hemp*).
* We will focus only on the main idea: *Paper made writing cheap and easy, which helped knowledge spread worldwide.*

#### Step 2: Write the Précis (30 words):
* **Title:** How Paper Spread Knowledge.
* **Précis:** **The invention of paper replaced heavy writing materials with a cheap, light alternative. This allowed books to be made easily, helping education and ideas spread rapidly around the world.**

## 3. Paragraph Writing: The TEEL Method

Whenever you write a paragraph in an essay, use the **TEEL** method to keep it organized and powerful.

### The TEEL Method Breakdown:
* **T — Topic Sentence (The Point):** The very first sentence that states the main idea of the paragraph.
* **E — Explanation (The Details):** Explain your topic sentence in more detail.
* **E — Example / Evidence (The Proof):** Give a fact, statistic, or real-life example to prove your point.
* **L — Link (The Wrap-up):** Connect the paragraph back to the main topic of your essay.

### Full TEEL Paragraph Example (Topic: Why reading is good for you):

* **[Topic Sentence]** **First, reading books regularly is a great way to make your brain stronger.**
* **[Explanation]** **When you read, your brain has to work hard to remember characters, follow plots, and picture scenes, which acts like an exercise workout for your mind.**
* **[Example/Evidence]** **For example, scientific studies show that children who read for just fifteen minutes a day score higher on vocabulary tests than those who do not.**
* **[Link]** **Therefore, reading daily is a simple yet effective tool for boosting your intelligence.**

## 4. Formal Business Letters

A formal letter is used when writing to teachers, principals, or business managers. We use the **Full Block Format**, which means every line starts at the left margin, and we leave a blank line between paragraphs instead of indenting.

### Layout of a Formal Letter:


[Your Name/Address]

[Date]

[Recipient's Name and Address]

[Subject Line]

[Salutation]

[Body Paragraph 1 - The Purpose]
[Body Paragraph 2 - The Details]
[Body Paragraph 3 - The Action/Close]

[Sign-off]
[Your Signature/Name]


### Full Example Letter:


Rohan Das
12 Park Street

Thank you for your time and help. I hope to hear from you soon.

Sincerely,

[Signature]

Rohan Das
Class 10, Roll Number 12

## 6. E-mail Etiquette (Netiquette)

Emails are fast, but they still need to be polite and professional.

### Bad Email (Do Not Do This)

> **Subject:** help
>
> hey teach, thx for class today. can u send the slides pls? need them now. bye

### Good Email (Do This Instead)

> **Subject:** Request for Today's Lecture Slides
>

>
> Thank you,
>
> Amit Sen

## 7. Practice Questions

### Part A: Précis Practice
Write a short précis (approx. 20 words) for the passage below. Remember to add a title.

* *Passage (60 words):*  
  "Cycling is one of the best exercises you can do. It builds muscle strength, helps your heart stay healthy, and burns lots of calories. In addition, riding a bicycle is completely environment-friendly because it does not produce any smoke or pollution. Therefore, choosing to ride a bike is good for both your health and the planet."

#### Answer:
* **Title:** The Double Benefits of Cycling.
* **Précis:** **Cycling is a great exercise that strengthens the heart and muscles. Since it causes no pollution, it benefits both personal health and the environment.**

### Part B: TEEL Method Check
Identify which sentence in the following paragraph is the **Example**:

* *"Regular exercise is important for physical health. It helps keep our muscles strong and our hearts working well. For instance, walking for thirty minutes every day reduces the risk of heart problems. Therefore, daily movement is key to a long life."*

#### Answer:
* The Example sentence is: **"For instance, walking for thirty minutes every day reduces the risk of heart problems."** (It uses the clue words "For instance" to introduce real-world proof).
